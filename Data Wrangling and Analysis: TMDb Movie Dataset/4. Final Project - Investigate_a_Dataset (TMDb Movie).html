Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

This data set contains information about 10,000 movies collected from The Movie Database (TMDb), including user ratings and revenue.

Column Names¶

  • id - A unique identifier for each movie.

  • imdb_id - A unique identifier for each movie on IMDB.

  • popularity - A numeric quantity specifying the movie popularity.

  • budget - The budget in which the movie was made.

  • revenue - The worldwide revenue generated by the movie.

  • original_title - The title of the movie before translation or adaptation.

  • cast - The name of lead and supporting actors.

  • homepage - A link to the homepage of the movie.

  • director - the director of the movie

  • tagline - Movie's tagline.

  • keywords - The keywords or tags related to the movie.

  • overview - A brief description of the movie.

  • runtime - The running time of the movie in minutes.

  • genre - The genre of the movie, Action, Comedy ,Thriller etc.

  • production_companies - The production house of the movie.

  • release_date - The date on which it was released.

  • vote_count - Number of people vote for a specific movie.

  • vote_average - Average ratings the movie recieved.

  • release_year - The year when the movie is released

  • budget_adj - The budget associated movie in terms of 2010 dollars.

  • revenue_adj - The revenue associated movie in terms of 2010 dollars.

Questions for Analysis¶

In the following analysis, we are going to find out answers for the following 4 questions. We will address these questions in the data analysis and conclusion sections.

  1. What are the top 4 movies with the highest budgets?

  2. What are the top 4 movies with the highest revenue?

  3. What are the top 4 most popular movies?

  4. Does the budget or revenue of a movie impact its popularity?

Below are the detailed steps taken to clean the TMDb dataset.

# Set up a function for plotting bar charts below
# Plot a bar chart for the top 4 entries in the DataFrame based on a specified value column.
def plot_top_4_bar_chart(df, title_col, value_col, x_label, y_label, chart_title):
    plt.figure(figsize=(11, 6))
    plt.bar(df[title_col], df[value_col])
    plt.xlabel(x_label)
    plt.ylabel(y_label)
    plt.title(chart_title)
    plt.xticks(rotation=45)
    plt.show()

From the results above, it's evident that the top 4 movies with the highest budgets, highest revenues, and highest popularity scores are distinct from each other. This observation leads us to think whether movie budget or movie revenue can impact popularity score.

Although no statistical tests have been conducted yet, the scatter plot (Budget vs Popularity) above visually suggests that there is no clear relationship between a movie's budget and its popularity score, indicating that popularity may not be directly influenced by budget.

Although no statistical tests have been conducted yet, the scatter plot (Revenue vs Popularity) above visually suggests that there is no clear relationship between a movie's revenue and its popularity score, indicating that popularity may not be directly influenced by revenue.

On the other hand, although no statistical tests have been conducted yet, the scatter plot (Budget vs Revenue) above visually suggests a positive relationship between a movie's budget and its revenue, indicating that higher budgets may be associated with higher revenues.
